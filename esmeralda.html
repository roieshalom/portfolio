Solo Project

A fortune teller on a business card,
powered by AI

Ask Esmeralda ➜

Ask Esmeralda started as a party trick. I wanted to build something that could live in the physical world and conjure a digital experience from a single tap. No app download. No login. Just a card, a phone, and a question.

The card has an NFC chip. You tap it, the browser opens, and Esmeralda is waiting. Ask her anything: a question about work, love, a decision you've been avoiding. She answers in character, part oracle, part entertainer, entirely in her own voice. The AI is Claude. The character is mine.

The Card

The best interface is no interface

I wanted the entry point to feel like magic. NFC cards are invisible technology. You tap and something happens. There's no icon to tap, no URL to remember, no app to install. The card is the product. The mystery starts before the page loads.

I designed the card to look like a tarot card, printed on matte black stock. The gold illustration is Esmeralda herself. On the back, just a tagline: "Ask me anything." No instructions needed.

The physical card. Tap it, and Esmeralda answers.

The Experience

Atmospheric by design

Every detail of the web experience is designed to sustain the illusion. The page opens dark, the typeface is old-world serif, and Esmeralda's responses arrive word by word, as if she's thinking. The UI disappears into the background. What's left is the conversation.

I wrote the system prompt as if it were a character bible. Esmeralda is cryptic but warm, confident but playful. She never breaks character. She doesn't say "As an AI language model." She says things like "The cards are rarely wrong about this."

The Funnel

Word of mouth as the growth engine

I give cards away at events, dinners, and meetings. One card produces multiple sessions. The person who receives it shows it to whoever's nearby. The physical object creates curiosity before anyone has typed a word.

After a reading, Esmeralda offers a share link. Not a prompt to share, just a link in the response, framed naturally. "If you want a second opinion, send this to someone you trust." Some people do. That's the loop.

Real usage data. Most sessions arrive through a shared link or a tapped card.
