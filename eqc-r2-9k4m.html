EQCapture inventory tracking

EQCapture is a mobile app that replaces paper, photos and spreadsheets with a single workflow for on-site equipment surveys in large commercial buildings. It guides surveyors through complex properties, captures equipment details in context and syncs everything directly into the BuildingMinds digital twin.

Plan and process

As Head of Design for BuildingMinds' App Factory, I led EQCapture as part of a core three-person team. I owned end-to-end design: field research with surveyors in Hamburg and Frankfurt, UX/UI and design system work, usability testing and stakeholder presentations, while mentoring the Product Manager in user-centred methods.

Project goals

  1. Replace fragmented paper, photo and Excel workflows with a single mobile app connected to the BuildingMinds platform.
  2. Reduce time and errors when capturing large volumes of equipment data across multi-floor buildings.
  3. Keep clear spatial context (building, floor, room) for every asset to support a reliable digital twin.
  4. Make surveyors' on-site work smoother by cutting repetitive typing and tedious post-processing.

Plan and process

  1. Discovery: Map the existing survey workflow end to end and shadow surveyors on real projects to uncover pain points and edge cases.
  2. Definition: Prioritise breaks in the process (lost context between photos and equipment, manual re-typing, lack of tracking) and turn them into core MVP flows.
  3. Design: Create task-focused journeys for selecting building → floor → space, capturing records, attaching photos and filtering via DIN-based equipment structures.
  4. Validation: Prototype key flows, test with surveyors using both paper and interactive prototypes, and iterate on speed, clarity and error handling.

Achievements and outcomes

  1. Designed an end-to-end mobile capture journey that connects on-site work directly with the BuildingMinds digital twin, replacing several disconnected tools with one focused app.
  2. Built a reusable mobile UI library aligned with the BuildingMinds design system, laying groundwork for future App Factory apps to share patterns and components.
  3. Achieved a concept that field feedback indicates can cut per-equipment capture time by up to around five times for typical assets, while improving visibility into survey progress across buildings and floors.
